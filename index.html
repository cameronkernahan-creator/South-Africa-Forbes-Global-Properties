The Land

A pristine wilderness
once walked by Zulu kings.

Thanda Safari is a formally proclaimed Protected Environment, set within iZulu, the Land of Heaven. In 1895, this land was originally proclaimed as one of the first nature reserves in Africa by the British Colonial Government under Queen Victoria. In 2002, Dan Olofsson purchased the land, rewilded it with all naturally occurring animals, and it was re-proclaimed a Protected Environment in 2015, 120 years later. No land claim exists over the estate and no communities were ever dispossessed.

Conservation & Community

Built for the land.
Built for its people.

The Thanda Foundation Trust, established in 2005, works in close partnership with the Zulu Royal Family, local tribal authorities, and government. Over 90% of the Thanda team is from neighbouring communities. The Foundation's flagship initiative, Star for Life, has reached more than 540,000 young people over 21 years.

Thanda is a founder member of Project Rhino KZN, an intensive anti-poaching collaboration, and runs an active cheetah breeding programme. Every owner directly sustains these efforts, because ownership here is not passive, it is a promise to the land.
